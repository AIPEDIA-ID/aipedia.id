# Creative — Hook Formulas, Angle Frameworks & Fatigue Signals

## Decision Rule
Use this file when the user asks to write ad copy, improve hooks, or when CTR is the diagnosed bottleneck.

## The Hook Formula (First 3 Seconds)

A hook must do ONE of these immediately:
1. **State the specific outcome** — "Saya dapat 47 leads dari Meta Ads dengan budget Rp300ribu."
2. **Call out the exact audience** — "Buat kamu yang sudah coba iklan tapi ROAS-nya masih minus..."
3. **State a provocative truth** — "Kebanyakan iklan gagal bukan karena budgetnya kecil."
4. **Create a curiosity gap** — "Satu hal yang saya ubah di landing page, CVR naik 2× dalam 3 hari."
5. **Validate a pain** — "Capek riset audience tapi iklan tetap nggak perform?"

## The 5 Core Ad Angles

| Angle | Best For | Example Hook |
|---|---|---|
| **Pain/Problem** | Cold audience who knows their problem | "Iklan kamu jalan, tapi nggak ada yang beli?" |
| **Desire/Dream** | Cold audience motivated by aspiration | "Bayangkan ROAS 4× tanpa tambah budget" |
| **Fear/Risk** | High-intent audience near decision | "Yang bikin iklan mahal bukan budget — tapi ini" |
| **Social Proof** | Warm audience needing validation | "300+ bisnis di Indonesia pakai cara ini" |
| **Curiosity/Mechanism** | Any audience interested in a new method | "Ada 1 tweak di copy iklan yang naikkan CTR 80%" |

## Creative Fatigue Signals

Detect creative fatigue when:
- Frequency > 3 AND CTR declining week-over-week.
- CPM increasing despite stable targeting.
- Comments/engagement ratio dropping (people are seeing it but ignoring it).

**What to do:**
1. Keep the offer/landing page. Change only the creative.
2. Change one element at a time: hook → format → angle.
3. Do NOT change targeting or budget when refreshing creative — isolate the variable.

## Creative Testing Priority

Test in this order — never test multiple variables at once:
1. **Hook/First Frame** — This impacts CTR the most.
2. **Format** — Static vs video vs carousel.
3. **Angle** — Pain vs desire vs social proof.
4. **Offer/CTA** — Only after hook and format are validated.

## Copy Structure for Meta Ads (Primary Text)

```
[Hook — Call out audience or pain in 1-2 sentences]

[Problem amplification — make the pain real, 2-3 sentences]

[Mechanism — introduce how the product solves it, 1-2 sentences]

[Proof — one specific number or testimonial, 1 sentence]

[Offer — what they get, 1 sentence]

[CTA — one specific action]
```

## Writing Rules
- Write at reading level of a 14-year-old. Simple words, short sentences.
- No jargon unless audience is experts (agencies, marketers).
- Always make the benefit concrete: "Hemat 3 jam sehari" bukan "Hemat waktu."
- One CTA per ad. Never "Klik link atau DM kami" — pick one.
- Keep primary text under 125 characters if possible for mobile preview.
# Landing Page — Evaluation Checklist & Conversion Principles

## Decision Rule
Use this file when: (1) user shares a LP URL or copy, (2) LP CVR is diagnosed as the bottleneck.
Evaluate each section. Score 🔴 (broken), 🟡 (improvable), 🟢 (strong).

## The 5-Section LP Evaluation Framework

### Section 1: Hero (Above the Fold)
**Question: Does a stranger understand the outcome within 3 seconds?**

Checklist:
- [ ] Headline states a specific outcome, not the product name.
- [ ] Sub-headline clarifies who it's for.
- [ ] CTA button is visible without scrolling.
- [ ] Hero image/video is relevant (not stock photo of unrelated people smiling).
- [ ] No confusion about what happens when you click the button.

Common failures:
- Headline is the brand name ("Selamat Datang di Toko ABC") instead of the benefit.
- CTA says "Submit" or "Daftar" instead of specific action ("Dapatkan Akses Sekarang").

---

### Section 2: Problem Frame
**Question: Does the LP make the reader feel understood before pitching?**

Checklist:
- [ ] Specific pain is named (not generic "pasti susah").
- [ ] Cost of inaction is clear ("kalau dibiarkan, ini yang terjadi...").
- [ ] Reader nods before product is introduced.

Common failures:
- Jumping straight to product features without validating pain.
- Pain is too vague — "banyak yang kesulitan dalam bisnis."

---

### Section 3: Mechanism / Product
**Question: Is it clear HOW the product solves the problem?**

Checklist:
- [ ] Product is introduced with a mechanism (HOW it works, not just WHAT it is).
- [ ] Benefits are concrete and outcome-focused, not feature-focused.
- [ ] No unexplained jargon.

Feature vs benefit example:
- ❌ Feature: "17 AI Specialists dengan prompt rahasia"
- ✅ Benefit: "17 ahli AI yang langsung jawab, nggak perlu belajar prompt dari nol"

---

### Section 4: Proof
**Question: Is there evidence that this works?**

Checklist:
- [ ] At least one specific testimonial with name, role, and result.
- [ ] Results are quantified where possible ("hemat 3 jam per hari", "ROAS naik dari 1.2 ke 3.4").
- [ ] Proof is relevant to the target audience (not testimonial from a different niche).

Proof hierarchy (strongest to weakest):
1. Specific result with numbers from similar customer.
2. Named testimonial with photo.
3. Social proof count ("2.300+ pengguna aktif").
4. Expert credential or media mention.
5. Generic testimonial ("bagus sekali").

---

### Section 5: Offer & CTA
**Question: Is the offer easy to say YES to?**

Checklist:
- [ ] Price is clearly stated (no "hubungi kami untuk harga").
- [ ] What the buyer gets is summarized clearly (checklist or bullet).
- [ ] Risk reversal is present (guarantee, free trial, or easy refund).
- [ ] Urgency/scarcity is genuine (not fake "Penawaran berakhir SEKARANG").
- [ ] CTA is action-oriented and specific.
- [ ] Checkout requires minimal steps.

## Quick LP Score Decision

- 4–5 sections 🟢 → LP is healthy. Look elsewhere (creative, offer price).
- 2–3 sections 🟡 → LP is dragging CVR. Prioritize fixing 🔴 sections first.
- 0–1 sections 🟢 → LP is the primary bottleneck. Rebuild hero and proof sections minimum.
# Offer — Value Equation, Risk Reversal & Urgency Tactics

## Decision Rule
Use this file when: (1) CTR is good, LP CVR is decent, but ROAS is still poor — the offer is likely not compelling enough. (2) User explicitly asks to improve the offer.

## The Offer is a Business Asset, Not a Sales Trick
A strong offer makes advertising easy. A weak offer requires amazing creative to compensate — and still may fail.
Before optimizing any ad, evaluate the offer against these criteria.

## The Value Equation

```
Perceived Value × Certainty of Outcome
─────────────────────────────────────── = Decision to Buy
Time Delay × Risk × Effort
```

To improve offer performance — do ONE of these:
1. **Increase Perceived Value**: Add bonuses, frame the price anchor, reframe what they're really getting.
2. **Increase Certainty**: Add testimonials, demonstrations, credentials, before/after.
3. **Reduce Time Delay**: "Langsung bisa digunakan", "Download instan setelah bayar."
4. **Reduce Risk**: Money-back guarantee, free trial, conditional refund.
5. **Reduce Effort**: Fewer steps to buy, simplified checkout, pre-filled info.

## The 4 Elements of a Strong Offer

| Element | Weak | Strong |
|---|---|---|
| **What they get** | "Akses ke software kami" | "17 template Meta Ads yang sudah terbukti + panduan penggunaan 30 menit" |
| **Price anchor** | "Rp147.000" | "Seharusnya Rp497.000 — hari ini Rp147.000" |
| **Risk reversal** | (none) | "Jika tidak cocok dalam 3 hari, refund no-questions-asked" |
| **Urgency/Scarcity** | "Beli sekarang" | "Harga naik ke Rp247.000 Jumat ini" (only if true) |

## Risk Reversal Options (Indonesia Market)

Use the appropriate type based on product:
- **Conditional refund**: "Refund jika output tidak sesuai klaim, dalam 3×24 jam." (Best for digital products)
- **Try before you buy**: Free sample/preview content.
- **Low-entry price**: Offer basic version at low price to build trust before upsell.
- **Social proof as risk reduction**: Enough testimonials = users feel safer buying.

**Important**: Never promise a refund you can't operationally deliver. It backfires badly.

## Urgency and Scarcity Rules

Only use urgency/scarcity if it's REAL:
- ✅ "Harga early bird berakhir Minggu ini" — valid if true.
- ✅ "Sisa 12 slot konsultasi bulan ini" — valid if there's a real limit.
- ❌ "Penawaran berakhir HARI INI" refreshed daily — users see through this, kills trust.
- ❌ Fake countdown timers — violates Meta policy and destroys credibility.

## Price Anchoring Techniques

1. **Original price cross-out**: "~~Rp247.000~~ → Rp147.000" — visual discount.
2. **Value stacking**: List everything included with individual values, show total > selling price.
3. **Cost comparison**: "Lebih murah dari 1× konsultasi dengan agency."
4. **Daily cost framing**: "Kurang dari Rp500/hari selama setahun."

## When the Offer is the Problem (Not the Ads)
Signs:
- CTR > 1.5% but CVR < 1% even with LP changes.
- High cart abandonment.
- Multiple LP variants tested, none converts.
- Users engage/DM but don't buy.

Diagnosis: The gap between ad promise and offer reality is too large, OR the offer isn't compelling enough at that price point to overcome risk.

Fix:
1. Add one strong risk reversal element.
2. Add social proof specific to the buyer's profile (same niche, same business size).
3. Reframe what they're getting — use outcome language, not feature language.
