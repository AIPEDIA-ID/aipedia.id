# Examples - Indonesian SME Meta Ads Case Patterns

## How to Use This File
These are pattern examples, not guaranteed outcomes.
Use them to illustrate concepts, suggest testing hypotheses, and help users recognize their situation.
Never present these as client case studies unless user specifically has similar data.

---

## Pattern 1: High CTR, Low CVR (LP Mismatch)

**Situation**: Digital product seller. CTR 2.1%, CPC Rp1.200, but LP CVR only 0.6%.

**Diagnosis**: Creative is working (CTR excellent, CPC low). Problem is the landing page.

**Common cause**: Ad promised "5 cara menghasilkan uang dari rumah" but LP talks about a generic business course. Expectation mismatch.

**Fix applied**: Rewrote LP hero section to mirror the specific hook from the ad. Sub-headline now addresses exactly what the ad promised. Added a specific testimonial from someone in a similar situation.

**Result pattern**: LP CVR recovered to 2.2% within 7 days. No creative changes needed.

**Lesson**: When CTR is high, never touch the creative. Audit why LP feels different from the ad.

## Pattern 2: Low CTR Despite Good Audience (Hook Problem)

**Situation**: Physical product. CTR 0.4%, CPC Rp5.200. Audience is interest-targeted (relevant), budget sufficient.

**Diagnosis**: Creative/hook problem. Audience is right, but the first 3 seconds aren't stopping the scroll.

**Original hook**: Product photo, no text overlay, caption starts with brand name.

**Hook rewrite**: Changed to video showing the problem (frustration/pain), then product as solution. Text overlay: "Kenapa [specific problem] padahal sudah [tried solution]?"

**Result pattern**: CTR improved to 1.8% with same audience. CPC dropped to Rp1.400.

**Lesson**: The first frame must trigger pattern interrupt or immediate recognition of pain. Product photos without context don't stop the scroll.

## Pattern 3: Everything Good, But ROAS Below Break-Even

**Situation**: CTR 1.7%, LP CVR 2.4%, but ROAS 1.1 (unprofitable). Selling Rp179.000 digital product.

**Diagnosis**: Offer/pricing problem. Funnel is healthy but the economics don't work.

**Math**: CPA at ROAS 1.1 with Rp179k product = ~Rp163k CPA. Product has ~70% margin = Rp125k gross profit. Net per sale = -Rp38k.

**Options evaluated**:
1. Increase price to Rp247k (test price sensitivity).
2. Reduce CPA by improving checkout CVR.
3. Add upsell to increase average order value.

**Fix applied**: Added one clear testimonial at checkout page to reduce checkout abandonment. Also added a Rp97k upsell (complementary product).

**Result pattern**: Checkout CVR +12%, average order value increased to Rp204k. ROAS improved to 2.1.

**Lesson**: Sometimes funnel is healthy but unit economics are the issue. Look at margins and AOV before declaring the campaign broken.

## Pattern 4: Ad Fatigue After Initial Success

**Situation**: Campaign running 5 weeks, ROAS was 3.2 in week 1–2, now 1.4. Frequency: 4.7.

**Diagnosis**: Classic ad fatigue. Same creative shown 4.7 times on average to the same audience.

**What NOT to do**: Don't change the offer, LP, or audience - all of these were working. Don't increase budget.

**Fix applied**: Kept the same LP, offer, and audience. Only refreshed creative:
- New video with different hook angle (switched from pain → desire).
- New static image with testimonial quote overlay.

**Result pattern**: ROAS recovered to 2.8 within 2 weeks after creative refresh.

**Lesson**: Ad fatigue = creative problem only. Don't touch what was working. Refresh only the creative.

## Pattern 5: Retargeting with No Warm Audience

**Situation**: New advertiser. Total budget Rp1.5jt/month. Wants to run retargeting campaign.

**Diagnosis**: Retargeting requires a warm audience to exist first. With Rp1.5jt/month, audience from cold traffic will be too small to retarget effectively.

**Recommendation**: Allocate 100% to cold prospecting first. Build audience data (LP visitors, video viewers, engagers) for minimum 30 days. Then introduce retargeting with 20–25% of budget.

**Lesson**: Retargeting without warm audience = ads shown to nobody. Fill the cold layer first.
