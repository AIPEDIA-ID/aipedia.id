# Competency Frameworks

## Competency Framework Structure

Competency model typically includes:
- **Core Competencies** - Required for all employees
- **Functional Competencies** - Role-specific skills
- **Leadership Competencies** - For managers and above

Each competency has levels:
- Level 1: Foundational
- Level 2: Intermediate
- Level 3: Advanced
- Level 4: Expert

---

## Core Competencies (All Roles)

### 1. Communication

**Level 1 (Foundational):**
- Expresses ideas clearly in writing and verbally
- Listens actively and asks clarifying questions
- Shares relevant information with team

**Level 2 (Intermediate):**
- Tailors communication to audience
- Provides clear and concise updates
- Handles difficult conversations professionally

**Level 3 (Advanced):**
- Influences through effective communication
- Presents complex information clearly to diverse audiences
- Facilitates productive discussions

**Level 4 (Expert):**
- Inspires and motivates through communication
- Builds communication protocols and standards
- Masters public speaking and presentation

---

### 2. Collaboration

**Level 1 (Foundational):**
- Works cooperatively with team members
- Shares workload fairly
- Supports team goals over individual goals

**Level 2 (Intermediate):**
- Builds positive working relationships
- Resolves minor conflicts constructively
- Contributes to team success

**Level 3 (Advanced):**
- Fosters collaboration across departments
- Mentors and supports team members
- Builds networks across the organization

**Level 4 (Expert):**
- Creates culture of collaboration
- Facilitates complex cross-functional projects
- Recognizes and rewards teamwork

---

### 3. Problem Solving

**Level 1 (Foundational):**
- Identifies routine problems
- Proposes basic solutions
- Follows established procedures

**Level 2 (Intermediate):**
- Analyzes root causes of issues
- Develops multiple solution options
- Implements solutions with guidance

**Level 3 (Advanced):**
- Solves complex, multi-faceted problems
- Anticipates potential issues
- Creates innovative solutions

**Level 4 (Expert):**
- Solves strategic, high-impact problems
- Develops problem-solving frameworks
- Trains others in problem-solving techniques

---

### 4. Adaptability

**Level 1 (Foundational):**
- Adapts to new tasks and priorities
- Learns new tools and processes quickly
- Handles minor changes positively

**Level 2 (Intermediate):**
- Thrives in changing environments
- Adjusts approach based on feedback
- Supports change initiatives

**Level 3 (Advanced):**
- Leads others through change
- Pivots strategies when needed
- Champions organizational change

**Level 4 (Expert):**
- Drives transformational change
- Builds resilient teams and systems
- Anticipates and prepares for disruption

- Provides feedback and coaching
- Removes blockers for team

**Level 2 (Manager):**
- Sets team goals and OKRs
- Develops team members
- Builds team culture

**Level 3 (Senior Manager):**
- Develops managers
- Builds succession planning
- Influences broader organization

**Level 4 (Director/VP):**
- Builds and leads organizations
- Defines culture and values
- Develops leadership pipeline

---

## Using Competency Frameworks

### For Hiring
- Define required competency levels for each role
- Use behavioral interview questions to assess competencies
- Create competency-based evaluation rubrics

### For Performance Reviews
- Rate employee competency level
- Identify strengths and gaps
- Create development plans

### For Career Development
- Show competency progression paths
- Identify training and development needs
- Plan career moves and promotions

### For Succession Planning
- Identify high-potential employees
- Build talent pipelines
- Prepare future leaders

## Sample Behavioral Interview Questions by Competency

### Communication
- "Tell me about a time you had to explain complex information to someone with no technical background."
- "Describe a situation where you had to deliver difficult feedback."

### Problem Solving
- "Walk me through a complex problem you solved recently."
- "Tell me about a time you identified an issue before it became a crisis."

### Collaboration
- "Describe a project where you had to work with people you didn't get along with."
- "Tell me about a time you helped a team member succeed."

### Adaptability
- "Describe a time when priorities changed rapidly."
- "Tell me about a time you had to learn a new skill quickly."

### Leadership
- "Tell me about a time you had to lead without authority."
- "Describe how you've developed your team members."

## Notes

- Competency levels should be role-specific and organization-appropriate
- Regularly review and update competency frameworks
- Use frameworks consistently across HR processes
- Train managers on using competency models effectively
- Competencies should align with organizational values and goals
