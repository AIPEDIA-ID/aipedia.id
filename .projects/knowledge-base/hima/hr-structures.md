# HR Structures & Frameworks

## HR Department Structure

### Small Startup (<20 employees)
```
Founder/CEO
    |
    └── HR Generalist (or handled by Ops/Founder)
```

### Growing Company (20-50 employees)
```
HR Manager
    ├── Talent Acquisition Specialist
    ├── HR Business Partner
    └── HR Coordinator (Admin)
```

### Mid-Size Company (50-200 employees)
```
Head of HR / HR Director
    ├── Talent Acquisition Team
    │   ├── Senior Recruiter
    │   └── Recruiter(s)
    ├── HR Business Partners (2-3)
    │   ├── HRBP - Engineering
    │   ├── HRBP - Sales/Marketing
    │   └── HRBP - Product/Operations
    ├── People Operations Team
    │   ├── HR Coordinator (Benefits)
    │   ├── HR Coordinator (Payroll)
    │   └── HR Coordinator (Onboarding)
    └── Learning & Development
        └── L&D Specialist
```

### Large Company (200+ employees)
```
Chief People Officer / VP of People
    ├── Talent Acquisition Department
    │   ├── Head of Talent Acquisition
    │   ├── Recruiters (by role)
    │   ├── Sourcing Specialist
    │   ├── Employer Brand Specialist
    │   └── Recruiting Coordinator
    ├── People Operations Department
    │   ├── Head of People Operations
    │   ├── Payroll Team
    │   ├── Benefits & Compensation
    │   ├── HRIS Specialist
    │   └── Compliance Specialist
    ├── HR Business Partners Department
    │   ├── Head of HRBP
    │   ├── Senior HRBPs (by department)
    │   └── HRBPs (by team)
    ├── Learning & Development Department
    │   ├── Head of L&D
    │   ├── Instructional Designer
    │   ├── Training Coordinators
    │   └── Leadership Development Specialist
    ├── Employee Experience Department
    │   ├── Head of Employee Experience
    │   ├── Internal Communications
    │   ├── Employee Relations Specialist
    │   └── Culture & Engagement Specialist
    └── People Analytics
        ├── Head of People Analytics
        └── Data Analyst(s)
```

## Performance Management System

### Structure

```
Goal Setting (Quarterly)
    ↓
Regular Check-ins (Monthly/Bi-weekly)
    ↓
Mid-Year Review
    ↓
End-Year Review
    ↓
Performance Rating & Compensation Review
```

### Components

**1. Goal Setting (OKRs/KPIs)**
- Individual goals aligned with company OKRs
- 3-5 key results per quarter
- Clear metrics and deadlines
- Regular progress tracking

**2. Regular Feedback**
- Weekly or bi-weekly 1:1s
- Real-time feedback
- Quarterly check-ins on OKRs
- Peer feedback

**3. Formal Reviews**
- Self-assessment
- Manager assessment
- 360 feedback (for senior roles)
- Calibration meetings (for consistency)

**4. Development Planning**
- Strengths identification
- Areas for improvement
- Training and development plan
- Career path discussion

**5. Outcomes**
- Performance rating
- Compensation adjustment
- Promotion consideration
- PIP if needed

## Onboarding Framework

### Pre-Boarding (Before Day 1)
- Send welcome email and information
- Collect required documents
- Set up email and accounts
- Prepare workspace and equipment
- Assign onboarding buddy

### Day 1
- Welcome breakfast/meeting
- Office/workspace tour
- Introduce team and key stakeholders
- Setup and training on tools/systems
- Review company values and culture
- Set up goals and expectations for first 30-60-90 days

### First Week
- Complete all necessary paperwork
- Meet with cross-functional team members
- Understand product/service
- Review processes and procedures
- Start on first assignments

### First Month
- Complete training programs
- Meet regularly with manager
- Begin working on deliverables
- Understand team dynamics
- Attend team meetings and events

### First 90 Days
- Complete probationary period
- Deliver first major project
- Performance checkpoint meeting
- Adjust goals if needed
- Transition to ongoing performance management

### Onboarding Checklist

**Documents & Compliance:**
- [ ] Employment contract signed
- [ ] Tax forms (W2/W4 or equivalent)
- [ ] Emergency contact information
- [ ] Direct deposit setup
- [ ] Company policy acknowledgment

**IT & Equipment:**
- [ ] Laptop/computer
- [ ] Monitor and peripherals
- [ ] Email account setup
- [ ] Slack/communication tools
- [ ] Access to necessary systems

**Training:**
- [ ] Company orientation
- [ ] Product/service training
- [ ] Tools and systems training
- [ ] Security and compliance training
- [ ] Role-specific training

**Team Integration:**
- [ ] Meet manager and team
- [ ] Introduction to key stakeholders
- [ ] Assigned onboarding buddy
- [ ] Attend team meetings
- [ ] Social events attended

**Goals & Expectations:**
- [ ] 30-day goals set
- [ ] 60-day goals set
- [ ] 90-day goals set
- [ ] First project assigned
- [ ] Success criteria clarified

## Career Path Framework

### Individual Contributor Track

```
Junior → Mid-Level → Senior → Lead → Principal → Architect
```

**Sample Progression:**

**Junior Developer**
- 0-2 years experience
- Learning fundamentals
- Works under supervision
- Completes assigned tasks

**Mid-Level Developer**
- 2-5 years experience
- Works independently
- Takes ownership of features
- Mentors juniors

**Senior Developer**
- 5-8 years experience
- Leads technical projects
- Designs complex systems
- Reviews code and guides others

**Lead Developer**
- 8-12 years experience
- Technical leadership
- Architecture decisions
- Cross-team collaboration

**Principal Developer**
- 12+ years experience
- Technical vision
- Innovation and research
- Industry thought leadership

---

### Management Track

```
Manager → Senior Manager → Director → VP → C-Level
```

**Sample Progression:**

**Manager**
- Manages 5-10 people
- Team delivery
- People development
- Budget management

**Senior Manager**
- Manages multiple teams
- Strategic planning
- Manager development
- Cross-functional alignment

**Director**
- Manages function/department
- Department strategy
- Organization design
- Budget and resource planning

**VP**
- Manages multiple functions
- Organizational strategy
- Executive leadership
- Large-scale initiatives

**C-Level**
- Company-wide impact
- Strategy and vision
- Stakeholder management
- Industry leadership

## Exit Interview Framework

### Exit Interview Questions

**Role & Responsibilities:**
- What were your primary responsibilities?
- How would you describe your day-to-day work?
- What was most/least satisfying about your role?

**Management & Leadership:**
- How would you rate your relationship with your manager?
- Did you receive adequate support and guidance?
- Was feedback provided regularly and constructively?

**Compensation & Benefits:**
- Were you satisfied with your compensation?
- How do you feel about the benefits package?
- Was the package competitive compared to market?

**Culture & Environment:**
- How would you describe the company culture?
- Did you feel supported and valued?
- What did you like most/least about the culture?

**Career Growth:**
- Did you see a clear career path?
- Were development opportunities provided?
- Did you feel challenged in your role?

**Reasons for Leaving:**
- What is your primary reason for leaving?
- Could anything have been done differently?
- Would you recommend this company as a place to work?

**Suggestions:**
- What could be improved?
- What would make you consider returning?
- Any other feedback?

## Offboarding Process

### Offboarding Checklist

**1. Notice Period (2 weeks to 1 month)**
- [ ] Formal resignation received
- [ ] Last day confirmed
- [ ] Team and stakeholders notified
- [ ] Knowledge transfer plan created

**2. Knowledge Transfer**
- [ ] Handover of responsibilities documented
- [ ] Training of replacement or team member
- [ ] Project status updates provided
- [ ] Key contacts and relationships documented

**3. Exit Interview**
- [ ] Exit interview scheduled and completed
- [ ] Feedback collected and documented
- [ ] Trends and insights identified

**4. Administrative Tasks**
- [ ] Resignation letter processed
- [ ] Final pay calculated
- [ ] Benefits and PTO processed
- [ ] Tax documents prepared

**5. Equipment & Access**
- [ ] Laptop and equipment returned
- [ ] Keys/access cards returned
- [ ] Email and accounts deactivated
- [ ] System access revoked
- [ ] Company property returned

**6. Offboarding Communication**
- [ ] Team departure announcement sent
- [ ] External clients/partners notified (if needed)
- [ ] Forwarding address and contact info updated
- [ ] LinkedIn network updated

## Notes

- Regularly review and update HR processes
- Ensure compliance with local labor laws (Indonesia: UU Cipta Kerja)
- Balance efficiency with employee experience
- Use data to inform HR decisions
- Foster a culture of continuous feedback and development
