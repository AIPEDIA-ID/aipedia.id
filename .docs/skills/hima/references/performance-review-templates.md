# Performance Review Templates

## Performance Review Structure

### Review Cycle
- **Self-Assessment**: Employee rates themselves
- **Manager Assessment**: Manager rates employee
- **360 Review** (optional): Peers, subordinates, other managers provide feedback
- **Discussion**: Face-to-face meeting
- **Goal Setting**: Set goals for next period

---

## Performance Review Rubric Scale

| Rating | Name | Description | Percentage |
|--------|------|-------------|------------|
| 5 | Exceptional | Exceeds expectations significantly, delivers outstanding results consistently | 10% |
| 4 | Exceeds Expectations | Consistently delivers above expected results | 20% |
| 3 | Meets Expectations | Delivers expected results reliably | 40% |
| 2 | Needs Improvement | Sometimes below expectations, needs development | 20% |
| 1 | Unsatisfactory | Consistently below expectations, requires serious improvement | 10% |

## Performance Review Best Practices

### For Managers

1. **Prepare thoroughly**
   - Review employee's self-assessment
   - Gather specific examples and data
   - Review previous goals and achievements

2. **Focus on behavior, not personality**
   - Describe what they did, not who they are
   - Use specific, observable examples
   - Avoid judgments or labels

3. **Balance positive and constructive feedback**
   - Recognize achievements first
   - Provide specific areas for improvement
   - Frame feedback as development, not criticism

4. **Make it a two-way conversation**
   - Ask employee for their perspective
   - Listen actively
   - Collaborate on solutions

5. **Set clear, actionable goals**
   - Make goals SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
   - Discuss how goals will be measured
   - Identify support and resources needed

### For Employees

1. **Complete self-assessment thoughtfully**
   - Be honest about strengths and weaknesses
   - Provide specific examples and achievements
   - Link accomplishments to business impact

2. **Prepare for the discussion**
   - Review your goals and achievements
   - Identify areas you want to develop
   - Think about questions to ask your manager

3. **Listen actively**
   - Take notes during the review
   - Ask for clarification if needed
   - Keep an open mind

4. **Take ownership of your development**
   - Identify training and growth opportunities
   - Propose projects that stretch you
   - Follow up on development plans

---

## Notes

- Conduct performance reviews quarterly or semi-annually
- Provide ongoing feedback between formal reviews
- Document conversations and agreements
- Link performance reviews to compensation and promotion decisions
- Ensure consistency and fairness across the organization
- Review and update templates regularly
